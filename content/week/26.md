## More Overall Notes

- Let’s look at [our milestones](/project/5/#minimum-viable-product): we should have a barebones-but-complete *MVP* today, moving into *refinement* next week.

- As we move more into design, let’s “put a pin” in any additional features and/or “bells and whistles.” Come back to these after the flow/look/feel is where you want it to be.

	If your functionality isn’t there yet, we’re behind—and that needs to come first to allow you to move to design! Two weeks! <!-- .note -->

- Consider the “storytelling” of your project—(good) stories have a start, middle, and an end. As we move more into your design, think of someone coming to your site/extension, using it, and then what its “end” state is. Does it feel resolved/finished?

- Don’t show visitors/us things we can’t yet do or use. If we have to do “step 1,” don’t show us “step 2” until we’ve done the first one. (Or show them *differently*, or *disabled*.)

	This is also a way to narrow your down your logic and edge-cases! And make the design less overwhelming. <!-- .note -->

- Also, [*affordances*](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Affordance)—what does this thing do? Make it obvious!

- In code, if your JS includes `element.style.property = someValue`, this is just an [inline style](/topic/css/#where-css-lives). Let’s mind our first-semester fundamentals about where our styles live (in our external stylesheets).

	If you want to pass a *dynamic* value to your stylesheet, you can set a [CSS variable](/topic/responsive/#briefly-css-variables) with `element.style.setProperty('--some-variable', someValue)`. <!-- .note -->

- When [attributing](https://typography-interaction-2526.github.io/syllabus/#aides-to-our-understanding) “AI” questions, be sure to properly *share* the conversation thread (not just copy your session URL). You can test this in an incognito window!

	Some links are also just broken? Check your attributions—a reminder:

	If you cannot furnish a link to your “conversation” and questions, you cannot use the tool nor its output. <!-- .quote -->

- Some of these conversation attribution links are not showing the full chat history—probably because the [*context window*](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Context_window) is limited. We need to see it from beginning to end! Start new, shorter, more *specific* conversations.

	 This is also a good practice to prevent LLM feedback loops, dead-end paths, and “hallucinations.” <!-- .note -->

- Generally we want to see narrower, more focused questioning—that does not cede your control/understanding to the LLM.
